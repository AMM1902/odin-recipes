Tangsuyuk (Korean Sweet and Sour Pork)

Description

Tangsuyuk is a very popular Korean Chinese dish. It is crispy deep fried pork coated with sweet and sour sauce. The pork is crunchy and the sauce is irresistible!

Tansuyuk / Tang soo yook / Tang su yuk ( 탕수육 ) is a very popular Korean Chinese dish known as Korean sweet and sour pork. The pork is double deep fried and it's crunchy. What's more, the sauce! hmm... The right balance of sweetness and sourness is totally addictive and irresistible! OK, I'm in love. Seriously, this is so good!

Ingredients

Meat

  • 500 g / 1.1 pounds pork loin, cut into thin strips (about 1 cm x 5 cm)
  • Some vegetable oil for cooking and deep frying

Note: You can use beef or chicken instead. Use lean and tender parts of the meat.

Marinade

  • 1 tablespoon rice wine (or any cooking wine)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger powder (or minced ginger)
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • A few sprinkles ground black pepper

Batter

  • 1 & 1/4 cup potato starch (or corn starch)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 2 egg whites

Fruit and Vegetables

  • 150 g / 5.3 ounces pineapple slices, cut into small chunks
  • 1/2 onion (70 g / 2.5 ounces), cut into small chunks
  • 1/2 green capsicum (50 g / 1.8 ounces), cut into small chunks
  • 1/2 red capsicum (50 g / 1.8 ounces), thinly sliced
  • 1/4 carrot (30 g/ 1 ounce), thinly sliced
  • 1/4 English cucumber (50 g / 1.8 ounces), thinly sliced

Note: Other popular fruit and vegetables options - earwood mushrooms and apple
